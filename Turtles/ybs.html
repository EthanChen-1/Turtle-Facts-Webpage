Yellow-Bellied Slider Facts

Scientific Name: Trachemys scripta scripta

Native Range: The South-East of the United States

Invasive Level: Highly-invasive species

Male yellow-bellied sliders can grow up to 9 inches long while females can grow up to 13 inches long.
Closely related to red-ear sliders.
Easily identifiable by their dark shells with yellow bands going across the scutes and their yellow underside with black spots.
This species is diurnal meaning they are most active during the day.
Unlike the red-ear slider, yellow-bellied sliders do not carry the salmonella virus.
Yellow-Bellied sliders can breathe through their butts in a process known as cloacal respiration.
They are also able to breathe without much oxygen in a process called anerobic respiration.
Yellow-Bellied sliders can live up to 40 years in captivity and in the wild.

Equipment for a Yellow-Bellied Slider

100+ Gallon tank for a fully grown yellow-bellied slider
500+ GPH Canister Filter
Water Heater
Basking Platform
Heat Bulb + Lamp
UVA + UVB Bulb + Lamp
Water Dechlorinator

How to care for your Yellow-Bellied Slider

Place heat Bulb and UVA + UVB bulbs above basking platform.
Make sure to fill up the water as much as possible, red ear sliders like to swim.
Water heater should be 74-83 degrees. You don't want your turtles to freeze but don't cook them either.
Don't use small pebbles for the substrate as yellow-bellied sliders will eat almost everything that fits into their mouth.
Feed younger turtles once a day and feed adults every 2-3 days.
Do water changes once a week with dechlorinated water.
Don't handle them too much as they can die from the stress of being over-handled.
Remember to replace UV bulbs every half a year.
