Red-Ear Slider Facts

Scientific Name: Trachemys scripta elegans

Native Range: The Southern Mid-West of the United States

Highly-invasive species

Red-ear slider's are one of the most invasive species of animals in the United States.
Closely related to the yellow-bellied slider.
Most red-ear sliders are average 6-8 inches in length with the bigger ones growing to 12+ inches.
They are easily identifiable by their red patch near their ears.
They are referred to as sliders because of how they appear to slide into water at any sign of danger.
Red-ear sliders are able to "breathe" through their butts in a process known as cloacal respiration.
This species is diurnal meaning they are most active during the day.
They can also breathe without much oxygen in a process known as anerobic respiration.
Red-ear sliders are omnivores with their diet consisting of insects, fish, and aquatic vegetation.
They are carriers of salmonella so becareful when handling red-ear sliders.
Lastly red-ear sliders can live upto 40 years in the wild.

Equipment for a Red-Ear Slider

100+ Gallon tank for a fully grown red-ear slider
500+ GPH Canister Filter
Water Heater
Basking Platform
Heat Bulb + Lamp
UVA + UVB Bulb + Lamp
Water Dechlorinator

How to care for your Red-Ear Slider

Place heat Bulb and UVA + UVB bulbs above basking platform.
Make sure to fill up the water as much as possible, red ear sliders like to swim.
Water heater should be 74-83 degrees. You don't want your turtles to freeze but don't cook them either.
Don't use small pebbles for the substrate as Red Ear Sliders will eat almost everything that fits into their mouth.
Feed younger turtles once a day and feed adults every 2-3 days.
They don't like to be handled and can die from the stress acquired from over-handling.
Do water changes once a week with dechlorinated water.
Remember to replace UV bulbs every half a year.
