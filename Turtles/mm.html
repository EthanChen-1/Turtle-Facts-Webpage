Mississippi Map Turtle Facts

Scientific Name: Graptemys pseudogeographica kohnii

Native Range: Southern Mid-West

Invasive Level:

Male mississippi map turtles can grow up to 5 inches and females double them in size growing up to 10 inches.
They like to swim in turbulent water.
Their most distinguishing feature is the saw like protrusions from the top of their shell, and the pattern of the shell is like that of the contour lines on a map.
Their diets consist mostly of crustaceans like snails and clams.
Mississippi map turtles can live up to 30 years in captivity.

Equipment for a Mississippi Map Turtle

125+ Gallon tank for a fully grown female mississippi map turtle, males can live in smaller 50-75 gallons
500+ GPH Canister Filter
Water Heater
Basking Platform
Heat Bulb + Lamp
UVA + UVB Bulb + Lamp
Water Dechlorinator

How to care for your Mississippi Map Turtle

Place heat Bulb and UVA + UVB bulbs above basking platform.
Make sure to fill up the water as much as possible, red ear sliders like to swim.
Water heater should be 74-83 degrees. You don't want your turtles to freeze but don't cook them either.
Don't use small pebbles for the substrate as mississippi map turtle will eat almost everything that fits into their mouth.
Feed younger turtles once a day and feed adults every 2-3 days.
Do water changes once a week with dechlorinated water.
Don't handle them too much as they can die from the stress of being over-handled.
Remember to replace UV bulbs every half a year.
