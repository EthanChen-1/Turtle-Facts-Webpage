Painted Turtle Facts

Scientific Name: Chrysemys Picta Picta

Native Range: Most of the United States

Invasive Level: Not Invasive

Painted turtles can grow up to 10 inches long.
Painted turtles can live up to 55 years in the wild.
The painted turtle is identifiable by the redish stripes that go all along its body.
Painted turtles hibernate during the winter months.
Some painted turtles have been known to live in brackish waters.
In the wild painted turtles main feed on insects and fish.
Painted turtles are the most widely spread native turtle species found in the United States.

Equipment for a Painted Turtle

100+ Gallon tank for a fully grown female painted turtle, 50+ for a male painted turtle
500+ GPH Canister Filter
Water Heater
Basking Platform
Heat Bulb + Lamp
UVA + UVB Bulb + Lamp
Water Dechlorinator

How to care for your Red-Ear Slider

Place heat Bulb and UVA + UVB bulbs above basking platform.
Make sure to fill up the water as much as possible, painted like to swim.
Water heater should be 74-83 degrees. You don't want your turtles to freeze but don't cook them either.
Don't use small pebbles for the substrate as painted turtles will eat almost everything that fits into their mouth.
Feed younger turtles once a day and feed adults every 2-3 days.
They don't like to be handled and can die from the stress acquired from over-handling.
Do water changes once a week with dechlorinated water.
Remember to replace UV bulbs every half a year.
