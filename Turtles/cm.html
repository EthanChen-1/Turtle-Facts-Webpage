Common Musk Turtle Facts

Scientific Name: Sternotherus odoratus

Native Range: Eastern United States

Invasive Level: Not Invasive

Common Musk turtles only grow upto 5 inches as an adult.
They are nocturnal and are active during the night.
They are not very strong swimmers, preferring slower waters.
Common Musk turtles can be identified by their larger heads and smaller bodies.
They are also known as stinkpots because they like to musk when threatened.
Common musk turtles are known to carry salmonella.
They are omnivores but will mostly only eat meat.

Equipment for a Mississippi Map Turtle

55 gallons for a full grown common musk turtles
500+ GPH Canister Filter
Water Heater
Basking Platform
Heat Bulb + Lamp
UVA + UVB Bulb + Lamp
Water Dechlorinator

How to care for your Common Musk Turtles

Place heat Bulb and UVA + UVB bulbs above basking platform.
You don't really need to fill the water up that high, common musk turtles struggle to swim.
Water heater should be 74-83 degrees. You don't want your turtles to freeze but don't cook them either.
Don't use small pebbles for the substrate as mississippi map turtle will eat almost everything that fits into their mouth.
Feed younger turtles once a day and feed adults every 2-3 days.
Do water changes once a week with dechlorinated water.
Don't handle them too much as they can die from the stress of being over-handled.
Remember to replace UV bulbs every half a year.
